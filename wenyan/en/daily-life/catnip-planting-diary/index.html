A Beginner's Journey to Growing Cat Grass (Continuously Updating) 🌱 ​

If this is your first time growing hydroponic cat grass and you're looking for some guidance, I recommend reading my previous articleGrowing Hydroponic Cat Grass for the First Time! A Fresh Salad for Your Cat 🌱 first. That's because this is a record of my first attempt at growing without knowing anything, so don’t follow my methods too closely! However, if you want to see how a newbie clumsily handles things, then come along!

Day 1: Sowing ​

Let’s first welcome our cat guest, the "Garbage Bag Super Cat"—Sooga from the Bear family 🎉🎉🎉

Next, I brought out the wheat seeds and hydroponic box I bought from Shopee 🎉

In the morning, I spread the whole packet of seeds into the container and added water until it nearly covered the seeds—later, I realized that the seeds shouldn’t be fully submerged when sowing, but this method would work if you were sprouting them. Clearly, I ended up soaking the seeds until the next day...

In the afternoon, the seeds sprouted!

I caught our cat guest sniffing the seeds in action.

Day 1 Summary

I sowed the seeds in the morning and placed them by the window, where they didn’t get direct sunlight, so everything was fine. By the afternoon, some seeds had already sprouted, and I’m excited to see them grow! 🌱

Don’t Do What I Did

It’s best to follow the first step inGrowing Hydroponic Cat Grass for the First Time! A Fresh Salad for Your Cat 🌱 by soaking the wheat seeds in water for 6-8 hours before planting and making sure the water just touches the seeds when sowing.

Day 2: Sprouting ​

You can see that the seeds are starting to sprout, but things are looking a little crowded...

Day 2 Summary

I just waited for the seeds to grow and changed the water because it had become cloudy. This time, I didn’t let the water submerge the seeds.

Day 3: Grouping ​

I divided them! ...Actually, I thought it was too crowded, so I moved the sprouted seeds to another container. I divided them into the "Lazy Group" and the "Healthy Group"—clearly, my random planting method led to a limited number of healthy seeds.

Day 3 Summary

It was really satisfying to see the green grass growing from the sprouted seeds, kind of like watching a child grow up—though it’s only the third day. ::: :::tip Don’t Do What I Did Newbies should avoid scattering too many seeds at once, or they’ll end up with a mix of healthy and dead seeds, which easily leads to mold growth!

Don’t Do What I Did

Newbies should avoid scattering too many seeds at once, or they’ll end up with a mix of healthy and dead seeds, which easily leads to mold growth!

Day 4: Growing Taller ​

Suddenly, they shot up!! I continued changing the water daily and gradually moved the sprouted seeds from the "Lazy Group" to the "Healthy Group." Some parts did develop mold, which I cleaned off.

Day 4 Summary

I kept the container on my desk while I worked all day, and I couldn’t believe how quickly it was growing right next to me! Even though I was watching, it felt both slow and fast at the same time.

Day 5: Color Changing ​

By the fifth day, they continued to grow in the morning. I changed the water again and moved more sprouted seeds from the "Lazy Group" to the "Healthy Group." And by evening...

In the late afternoon on Day 5, I noticed that the light green grass was turning into a darker green, and they grew visibly taller. Some seeds from the "Lazy Group" were still sprouting.

Day 5 Summary

I realized that even changing the water once a day wasn’t enough—it’s best to change it two or three times a day to avoid the water smelling...let’s say, unpleasant. My roommate said, "It smells like a spoiled lunchbox." So, after I changed the water again, both my roommate and the plants seemed much happier!

Day 6: Stabilizing ​

Here’s a picture of the "Lazy Group" and "Healthy Group" together. The "Healthy Group" has now taken up half the container’s space (mainly because I consolidated the "Lazy Group" into 1/2 of the container). But even by Day 6, some slower-growing seeds are still alive, so I haven’t given up on them yet. Maybe they’ll sprout—I blame this on not soaking the seeds beforehand.

Day 6 Summary

I’ve heard that hydroponic plants are usually ready for harvesting on Day 6, but things aren’t that simple in reality! I suspect it’s because I didn’t add any nutrients. But I’m curious to see how they grow without pre-sprouting or nutrient solutions—just daily water changes.
